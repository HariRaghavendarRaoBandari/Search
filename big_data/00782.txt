Non-stationary null dust in a spherically symmetric spacetime is studied in the context of a
general-covariant Hoˇrava-Lifshitz gravity. The non-minimal coupling to matter is considered in the
post-Newtonian approximation (PPN) in the infrared limit. The aim of this paper is to see if a
general relativistic spacetime of a shearing null dust-like ﬂuid can be a solution of Hoˇrava-Lifshitz
theory in the infrared limit. We have shown that this solution admits the process of gravitational
collapse leaving a singularity at the end. These solutions have, qualitatively, the same temporal
behavior as the dust collapse in Einstein theory. We have also found other possible solutions,
representing an expansion behavior that is not found in General Relativity (GR). This solution
might represent a repulsive phantom energy in GR.

I.

INTRODUCTION

Our current best description of gravity is provided by
Einstein’s General Relativity (GR) proposed exactly a
century ago. While this is a classical ﬁeld theory, the
other three fundamental interactions are quantum ﬁeld
theories. The construction of a quantum theory of gravi-
tation is one of the unsolved problems in modern physics.
The biggest challenge is the lack of any observational or
experimental results for quantum eﬀects of gravity. For
this reason, the criteria that a new candidate theory of
quantum gravity (if it is really necessary to complete the
description of the Nature) must fulﬁll are constrained
by mathematical self-consistency, ability to reproduce
observable results conﬁrming General Relativity, and ﬁ-
nally, making non-trivial predictions that may possibly
be tested in near future.

The most recent such candidate for quantum gravity
is Hoˇrava-Lifshitz (HL) theory [1], inspired by ideas from
condensed matter physics [2]. This has drawn a lot of
interest primarily because it can overcome the perturba-
tive non-renormalizability of quantized Einstein gravity.
This is accomplished at the expense of breaking Lorentz
invariance at very high energies, while restoring it at low
energies. Unfortunately, the original formulation of HL
theory suﬀered from a number of serious problems which
led to the development of various non-trivial modiﬁca-

tions [3–6]. One of the promising modiﬁcations is the one
without the so called projectability condition and with an
additional Abelian gauge symmetry, which in particular
solves the problem of unphysical scalar graviton [7, 8].
Due to the extensive works in this area, we will not be
able to cite all the important papers, but we suggest the
reader to reference [9] and the references therein.

In order to establish the physical relevance of HL grav-
ity, it is very important to check that it reproduces the
known physically feasible properties of standard GR at
low energies. To this end, the black holes and the gravi-
tational collapse were investigated in [10]. Lin et al [11],
have proposed a universal coupling between the gravity
and matter in the framework of HL theory with an ex-
tra U(1) symmetry for both the projectable and non-
projectable cases. Using this universal coupling they
have studied the PPN approximations and obtained the
PPN parameters in terms of the coupling constants of
the theory. Following this, we have checked whether
the non-projectable version of HL gravity with the ex-
tra U(1) gauge symmetry admits Vaidya-type solutions
in the low energy limit [12], without any coupling with
matter. The answer is negative and we have also shown
that there is not an analogy of the Vaidya’s solution in
the HL theory without projectability and with minimal
coupling [13]. This led us to an important conclusion that
in order to establish consistency with GR at low energies,
the gauge ﬁeld associated with the extra U(1) gauge sym-
metry of the enlarged non-projectable HL gravity should
have some interaction with the pure radiation/matter
generating the Vaidya spacetime geometry. On the other
hand, we have also studied non-stationary dust spheri-
cally symmetric spacetime, in projectable version of gen-
eral covariant theory of the HL theory, with non-minimal
coupling with matter, in the post-newtonian approxima-

tion (PPN) and in the infrared limit [14]. We have shown
that this solution admits a process of gravitational col-
lapse, leaving a singularity at the end. This solution has,
qualitatively, the same temporal behavior as the dust col-
lapse in GR. However, we have also found a second pos-
sible solution, representing an expansion behavior that is
not found in GR.

Considering the previous results, our goal

in this
present work is to revisit the null dust solution in the
projectable case of HL theory, but without using the
Vaidya’s spacetime. The paper is organized as follows.
In Section II, we present a brief introduction to HL the-
ory with coupling with matter [11].
In Section III, we
study the null dust solution with shear in the infrared
limit. In Section IV, we discuss the results. Finally, in
the Appendix A, we present some quantities necessary in
the ﬁeld equations of the theory with coupling [11].

V. CONCLUSION

We have studied the spherically symmetric spacetime
ﬁlled with a null dust ﬂuid in the framework of general
covariant HL theory with the non-minimal coupling [11],
in the PPN approximation of the infrared limit. We have
analyzed if such a solution can be described in the general
covariant HL theory of gravity [7, 8]. Although we do not
have a realistic model, it is shown that we can describe
the gravitational collapse as in the case of GR, although
representing another kind of matter. Using non-minimal
coupling with matter, we have seen that the solution ad-

mits gravitational collapse, leaving a singularity at the
end (tc > t > ∞). Note that we have also found other
possible solutions for −∞ < t < tc, representing an ex-
pansion behavior that is not expected in GR.
In order to compare, we have studied the same metric
in GR and have found that the solution has a similar,
but not the same behavior in both theories.
It is sur-
prising, however, that metric describes a matter that is
not a dust-like ﬂuid in GR, instead we have a repulsive
phantom energy ﬂuid with anisotropic pressures. Hence,
our results strongly suggest that the same metric can rep-
resent diﬀerent ﬂuids in HL and GR theories. Another
surprise is that for λ = 1, even in the infrared limit the
