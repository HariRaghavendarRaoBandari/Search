Such products are called shuﬄe products.

To treat the shuﬄe products of multiple zeta values formally, we adopt the
algebraic setting as in [5]. Let A = {x, y} be an alphabet with two non-commutative
letters, and let A∗ be the set of all words on A with the empty word 1. We denote
by h the non-commutative Q-polynomial algebra generated by the set A, and by
h0 the subalgebra

h0 = Q1 + xhy.

As rational vector spaces, h is spanned by A∗ and h0 is spanned by 1 and words
starting from x and ending with y. The shuﬄe product X on h is deﬁned by
Q-bilinearity and the rules:

1 X w = w X 1 = w,

aw1 X bw2 = a(w1 X bw2) + b(aw1 X w2),

for all letters a, b ∈ A and all words w, w1, w2 ∈ A∗. Under the shuﬄe product, h
becomes a commutative Q-algebra, and h0 is also a subalgebra.

We deﬁne a Q-linear map ζ : h0 → R by ζ(1) = 1 and

ζ(xk1 −1yxk2−1y · · · xkn −1y) = ζ(k1, . . . , kn),

where n, k1, k2, . . . , kn are positive integers with k1 > 1. Then it is easy to know
that the map ζ : (h0, X ) → R is an algebra homomorphism (see [5] for example).
In other words, we have

ζ(w1 X w2) = ζ(w1)ζ(w2)

for any w1, w2 ∈ h0. Hence to get shuﬄe product formulas of multiple zeta values,
one can treat the shuﬄe products of h0 ﬁrst. For example, since

xy X xy = 2xyxy + 4x2y2,

applying the map ζ, we get the shuﬄe product formula ζ(2)ζ(2) = 2ζ(2, 2)+4ζ(3, 1)
proved above.
