1. Introduction

The neutron density becomes very diﬀuse and single-particle spectrum shows similarity
of harmonic-oscillator as we approach towards neutron-drip line [1, 2]. This eﬀect we
can see at N = 40 shell gap. Recently intruder conﬁgurations are found in some neutron
rich nuclei around this shell gap [3, 4, 5, 6, 7]. Thus nuclei in this region are of great
interest and many investigations have been conducted for them [8, 9, 10].

Sorlin et al. [11], observed the experimental beta decay half-lives of neutron rich
57−59Ti, 59−62V, 61−64Cr, 63−66Mn, 65−68Fe and 67−70Co isotopes around N = 40 at
GANIL. They also compared experimentally observed half-lives of V and Cr isotopes
with QRPA calculation. The QRPA calculations for 58,59Cr, 61−63Mn, 63−64Fe, 65−67Co
and 67,69Ni with folded-Yukawa single-particle potential are reported in ref.
In
their work they have also compared the results with Bender et al., [13] based on Nilsson
potential. Using RIBF facility at RIKEN, the half-lives of twenty neutron-rich nuclei
with Z = 27 − 30 are reported in ref. [14]. In their work a sizable magicity reported for
both the proton number Z = 28 and the neutron number N = 50 in 78Ni, it is observed
that a sudden shortening of half-lives of the nickel isotopes beyond N = 50. Although
it is not pronounced in the Cu-Ge-Ga chains. The half-lives results from LISE2000
spectrometer at GANIL for 71Co and 73Co are reported in ref.
[15]. First time half-
lives of 77,78Ni experimentally measured by Hosmer et al.
[16], at NSCL, MSU. The
beta decay half-lives of 38 very neutron-rich Kr to Tc isotopes are measured at RIKEN
reported in ref. [17].

[12].

Despite the progress of experimental side, we need theoretical estimates for half-
lives for neutron rich nuclei, especially those belonging to the island of inversion.
These calculations are based on allowed GT-transitions. Many theoretical calculations
from quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) based on the Hartree-Fock
Bogoliubov method [18] or semiempirical global models [19, 20, 21] available in the
literature. These calculations underestimate the correlation among the nucleons which
predict GT-strength at low-energies. The shell model results able to predict half-lives
close to experimental data for neutron rich very well [22, 23, 24, 25]. Recently shell model
calculations for the β −-decays of Z = 9 − 13 nuclei are reported by Li and Ren [26]. The
motivation of the present work is to study β −-decays properties of Z = 20 − 30 nuclei,
some of which are within the island of inversion, for recently available experimental data
using nuclear shell model.

We organize, present work as follows.

In section 2, we present the formulas for
the calculation of β − decay half-lives. Shell model spaces, eﬀective interactions and the
quenching factors we adopted in our calculations are reported in section 3. In section
4, we present theoretical results along with experimental data wherever it is available.
Finally, summary and conclusions are drawn in section 5.

3. Hamiltonian and quenching factor

In the present work we perform calculation for f p and f5/2pg9/2 shell nuclei. We perform
calculation for f p shell nuclei using KB3G eﬀective interaction [33], while in the case
of Ni, Cu and Zn for f5/2pg9/2 model space we use JUN45 eﬀective interaction using
shell model code NuShellX [34]. The KB3G [33] interaction is extracted from the KB3
interaction by introducing mass dependence and reﬁning its original monopole changes
in order to treat properly the N = Z = 28 shell closure and its surroundings. The
JUN45, which was recently developed by Honma [35], is a realistic interaction based on
the Bonn-C potential ﬁtting by 400 experimental binding and excitation energy data
with mass numbers A = 63 - 96. We perform full-ﬂedged calculation, but for few f p
shell nuclei we freeze 2 - 8 neutrons in f7/2 orbital.

5. Summary

In the present work using shell model we have reported the half-lives, logf t values, and
branching fractions of nuclei with Z = 20−30 and N ≤ 50. We perform calculation for f p
shell nuclei using KB3G eﬀective interaction. In the case of Ni, Cu and Zn for f5/2pg9/2
model space we use JUN45 eﬀective interaction. The comparison with experimental
results of excitation energies, logf t values, half-lives, Q-values and branching fractions
for most of nuclei show good agreement with the available experimental data. Some of
the concerned nuclei are belonging to the island of inversion, such as 63−66Mn and 67Co,
and their β-decays are well reproduced by the calculations. The present shell model
results will add more information to the earlier QRPA results due to M¨oller et al. [19].
