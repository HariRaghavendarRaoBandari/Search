Violations of CP T symmetry and Lorentz invariance are searched for by studying
interference eﬀects in B0 mixing and in B0
S and
s → J/ψ K+K− decays are recorded by the LHCb detector in proton–proton
B0
collisions at centre-of-mass energies of 7 and 8 TeV, corresponding to an integrated
luminosity of 3 fb−1. No periodic variations of the particle-antiparticle mass diﬀer-
ences are found, consistent with Lorentz invariance and CP T symmetry. Results are
expressed in terms of the Standard Model Extension parameter ∆aµ with precisions
of O(10−15) and O(10−14) GeV for the B0 and B0
s systems, respectively. With no
assumption on Lorentz (non-)invariance, the CP T -violating parameter z in the B0
s
system is measured for the ﬁrst time and found to be Re(z) = −0.022± 0.033± 0.005
and Im(z) = 0.004 ± 0.011 ± 0.002, where the ﬁrst uncertainties are statistical and
the second systematic.

Lorentz invariance and the combination of charge conjugation, spatial inversion and time
reversal (CP T ) are exact symmetries in the Standard Model (SM) of particle physics,
and are deeply connected in any quantum ﬁeld theory [1]. Quantum theories that aim
to describe Planck-scale physics, such as string theory, might break these fundamental
symmetries [2].
Interference eﬀects in the mixing of neutral mesons are sensitive to
violations of CP T symmetry, and may provide a window to the quantum gravity scale [3].
Lorentz and CP T violation can be described in a low-energy, eﬀective ﬁeld theory, as done
in the framework of the Standard Model Extension (SME) [4, 5]. The SME parameters
describing the violations are expected to be suppressed by powers of the Planck scale [6].
As the CP T -violating parameters also break Lorentz symmetry, they depend on the four-
velocity of the neutral mesons in an absolute coordinate frame. The SME parameters
for the B0 and B0
s systems can be measured with a time-dependent analysis of the decay
s → J/ψ K +K−, using the four-velocity of the B mesons [7].

wide range of frequencies is scanned by means of the periodogram method. A periodogram
gives the spectral power P (ν) of a frequency ν in a signal sampled at discrete, not
necessarily equidistant, times. In this analysis, the Lomb-Scargle periodogram [26] is used,
as in the BaBar measurement of ∆aB0

a wide range around the sidereal frequency, from 0.03 solar-day−1 to 2.10 solar-day−1. The
